EGYPTIENNE TYPE STUDY

These two spreads are dedicated to the the study of the slab serif typeface Egyptienne. Englishman Vincent Figgins introduced the first slab serif, also referred to as the Egyptian typeface, in 1815 as a result of a confluence of events from the previous decade. The Swiss contemporary graphic designer, typographer, and type designer, Adrian Frutiger, brought to life the typeface Eygptienne F in 1956. It was designed for the leading 19th century French type foundry Deberny and Peignot. It was the first typeface created exclusively for phototypesetting and offset lithography printing.
